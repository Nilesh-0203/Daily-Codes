<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/max-sum-subarray-by-removing-at-most-one-element/1">Max Subarray Sum by Removing At Most One</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO" style="--text-color: var(--problem-text-color);"><p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Given an array&nbsp;arr[], find the maximum sum of a non-empty subarray. You are allowed to skip&nbsp;at most one element&nbsp;in the subarray.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Note:</strong>&nbsp;</span><span style="font-size: 14pt;">After skipping the element, the subarray must still be non-empty.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Examples:</strong></span></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>arr[] = [1, 2, 3, -4, 5]
<strong>Output: </strong>11<strong>
Explanation: </strong>We can get maximum sum subarray by skipping -4.</span></pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>arr[] = [-2, -3, 4, -1, -2, 1, 5, -3]
<strong>Output: </strong>9<strong>
Explanation: </strong>We can get maximum sum subarray by skipping -2 as [4,-1,1,5] sums to 9, which is the maximum achievable sum.</span></pre>
